Frequently Asked Questions

📁 Artwork & File Types

What file formats do you accept?

We accept PC files in the following formats: TIF, BMP, AI (Adobe Illustrator), FH (Freehand), JPG (high resolution only). EPS files are preferred.

How do I send my artwork?

Email to concord@comcast.net. Compress files using ZIP. We also accept 3.5" floppy, Zip Disk, or Optical Disk. Please include your order number, name, and phone number with all fonts and imported images.

🎨 Artwork Tips

How can I reduce art charges?

  • Save in PC format (not Mac)
  • No gradients if cutting from vinyl
  • All objects must be CLOSED shapes
  • No lines thinner than 1/8"
  • Convert "fat lines" to outlines
  • Don't compress files under 1MB
  • Convert text to curves/outlines before exporting

🖼 Vector vs Bitmap

How do I know if I have a vector file?

BMP, JPG, GIF, and TIF are always bitmap files. Use "Wireframe" view (Corel) or "Artwork" view (Adobe) — if colors disappear and you see lines, it's a vector file.

Advantages of vector files: Can be scaled to any size without loss of resolution. Take up less storage space. Minimize art charges.

Bitmap resolution guide: 1MB per sq ft = magazine quality. 2.5MB per sq ft = near photo quality.

📏 The Science of Signs

How big should my text be?

For every 50 feet of viewing distance, increase text size by 1 inch.

Text size guide by traffic speed:

  • Foot traffic: 2-4" text
  • Under 45mph: 5"+ text minimum
  • 55-60mph: 8"+ text recommended

Moving audiences need 3-4 seconds to process 6-8 content groupings. Keep it simple!

Care & Installation Guides

🪧 Banner Care

Banner Installation & Care

Installation: Use strong ropes, bungee, snap hooks, or cable. Pull ropes horizontally — not diagonally. Keep tension in all four corners. Excess flapping causes tears, cracking, and delaminating.

Storage: Always roll your banner like a poster. Never fold it in half.

Durability: Many months when hung correctly with tension from all four corners and checked regularly. Polly banner material has tear strength of 30 lb per inch.

🧲 Magnetic Sign Care

Magnetic Sign Care

Application: Hold best on flat surfaces. Do NOT pull to realign once on surface — magnet may stretch.

Cleaning: Remove and clean both magnet and surface with mild detergent monthly. For vehicles, we recommend daily removal to protect clear coat.

Storage: Store FLAT. Never magnet to magnet. Keep in a clean, dry environment.

✂️ Vinyl Application

Vinyl Application Guide

Temperature: Apply when air and surface temperatures are above 55°F.

Surface Prep: Clean with Windex or 2:1 water/isopropyl alcohol mix. Dry thoroughly.

Application: Lay sign upside-down, peel backing, spray soapy water on surface, apply vinyl, squeegee out water, remove tape.

🧼 Surface Prep Guide

Surface Preparation

Plastic & Glass: Wipe clean with window cleaner. Remove dried matter with razor blade.

Painted Metals: Clean with Naphtha, Xylol, or Lacquer Thinner. Dry before solvent evaporates. Fresh paint — wait 72 hours.

Interior Painted: Use household cleaner (Fantastic or 409). Fresh paint — wait 72 hours.

Temperature: 45-90°F for best results. High humidity (90%+) may affect application.

🔧 Materials Needed

Vinyl Application Materials

  • Small cutting knife (Exacto knife ideal)
  • Spray bottle with soapy water (3 drops soap to 1 pint water)
  • Plastic squeegee (supplied)
  • Dry towel
  • 2" roll of masking tape

Step-by-Step:

  1. Tack graphic to surface with masking tape
  2. Apply horizontal hinge tape strip at top
  3. Remove liner, spray surface with soapy water
  4. Squeegee from top down, working center to edges
  5. Remove pre-mask (spray with water to loosen)
  6. Squeegee out remaining bubbles with liner paper
